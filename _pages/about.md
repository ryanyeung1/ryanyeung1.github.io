I am a MASc graduate in Biomedical Engineering from the University of British Columbia in Vancouver, Canada. I worked in the Robotics and Control Lab under the supervision of Prof. Tim Salcudean.

My research focused on developing a novel approach to model-mediated teleoperation for tele-robotic ultrasound that incorporated point cloud modelling and stiffness estimation for haptic feedback. My work was part of the human teleoperation project which aims to improve access to medical ultrasound in remote communities. I also led the clincal validation of human teleoperation, collaborating with clinicians and remote Indigenous communities.

I previously received a BASc in Biomedical Engineering from the University of British Columbia. During this time, I also worked as a mechatronics engineering co-op at Aspect Biosystems and a test engineering co-op at Verathon.
