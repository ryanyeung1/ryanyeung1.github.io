Video laryngoscopes give medical providers view of the airway for faster and more reliable intubation. To help keep up with medical demand during the COVID-19 pandemic, I designed two electromechanical systems to automate two quality control procedures.

### Hinge Cycle Testing
I collaborated with two other co-op test engineers to design a system to repeatedly rotate the hinge of a portable video laryngoscope monitor through its full range of motion. I designed the electrical and firmware components to control and power six modules in parallel. I also integrated a user interface with an LCD screen and rotary encoder, and a light sensor to measure the monitor screen brightness to detect failure.

### Connector Cycle Testing
I designed a crankshaft system to test the reliability of the laryngoscope connector. I modelled all the components in SolidWorks and used SLA 3D printing to fabricate the parts. I also implemented the embedded control and user interface for automated operation and monitoring of the test progress.
